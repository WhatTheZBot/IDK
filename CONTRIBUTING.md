# Contributing to the Mr Niftyfire Website

Thank you for your interest in the Mr Niftyfire Website. We welcome community contributions and appreciate your time and effort in helping improve the project. Before getting started, please take a moment to review these guidelines.

## 1. [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

I want this project to be a welcoming space for everyone. By contributing, you agree to follow our [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and help keep the community respectful and inclusive.

## 2. How to Contribute

I welcome contributions in various forms, including:

- Bug fixes
- Documentation improvements
- Design enhancements
- Media and content updates

If you would like to add major features or redesigns, please reach out to the Mr Niftyfire team via [email](mrniftyfireofficial@outlook.com) to discuss.  

To contribute, follow these steps:

- **Fork** the repository and create a new branch.
- **Make your changes**, ensuring they follow our standards (§4 below).
- **Preview your changes** to make sure everything displays correctly.
- **Submit a pull request (PR)** with a clear description of the changes.
- A maintainer will review your PR, suggest any necessary changes, and merge it once approved.

### Commit Messages

Use clear and descriptive commit messages.


## 3. Legal Terms

By submitting a contribution, you represent and warrant that:

- It is your original work.
- You grant the Mr Niftyfire Website maintainers the right to use, modify, and distribute it under the [MIT license](./LICENSE.md); and
- If your contribution includes third-party material, it must be MIT-compatible and properly attributed.

Where permitted by law, you waive any moral rights in your contribution (e.g., the right to object to modifications). If such rights cannot be waived, you agree not to assert them in a way that interferes with the website’s use of your contribution.

## 4. Coding and Content Standards

To maintain a consistent website:

- Follow existing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript conventions.
- Keep images optimized for web use.
- Document your changes clearly.
- Avoid unnecessary dependencies or scripts.

## 5. Reporting Issues

If you find a bug or want to request a feature:

- Open an issue on the repository (include steps to reproduce, your browser, and Mr Niftyfire Website version/date).
- Describe the expected vs actual behavior.
- Suggest improvements if possible.

## 6. Recognition

We value all contributors. If your contribution is merged, you will be credited in the project’s contributor list, including contributions of all kinds—code, content, design, testing, and more.
